\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end deep-learning system that translates images of printed
monophonic staff notation directly into numbered musical notation (Jianpu),
bypassing the intermediate MusicXML/MIDI formats and hand-written grouping
heuristics of conventional Optical Music Recognition (OMR) pipelines. A
Vision-Encoder-Decoder ingests a score image and a Transformer decoder emits four
\emph{decoupled} token streams---type, pitch, rhythm, and attribute---so that
pitch and rhythm accuracy can be measured independently; a post-processor maps
the streams to Jianpu, and the whole pipeline is shipped as an interactive demo.
We train and evaluate exclusively on a fully synthetic corpus built with
\texttt{music21} and \texttt{verovio}, whose generator emits the four label
streams as ground truth in lock-step with the rendered image. Three findings
emerge. (i)~An ImageNet-pretrained ViT encoder with an autoregressive (AR)
decoder solves the task almost perfectly (symbol error rate, SER, $0.0029$), a
from-scratch ResNet encoder under the same AR decoder \emph{structurally fails to
learn pitch} ($\approx0\%$), yet attaching a BiLSTM + per-head CTC decoder to the
\emph{same} ResNet features recovers a perfect SER of $0.0000$---showing the
bottleneck is the AR monotonic alignment over $1$-D column features, not the
convolutional encoder. (ii)~In a sample-efficiency sweep, CRNN+CTC reaches usable
quality from $5{,}000$ samples while the ViT-AR pitch head does not begin to learn
until $50{,}000$, contradicting the expectation that ImageNet pretraining helps in
the low-data regime. (iii)~A photo-augmentation fine-tune more than halves SER on
real phone photographs of scores ($0.634\!\to\!0.258$), and a leave-one-out
ablation isolates geometric (camera-angle) augmentation as the dominant factor.
\end{abstract}
